The 16 Best Beach Sandals of 2024

As a native of Southern California, I came to understand a few things at a young age: Always lather yourself up in sunscreen (even on overcast days), make sure your swimsuit isn’t see-through before wearing it in public, and never, ever make beach sandals an afterthought. Anyone who has ever trudged across a beach in a pair of cheap, drugstore thongs, tennis shoes, or expensive suede sandals understands this all too well. Investing in a few styles of beach sandals can save you from blisters between the toes, burned soles, ruined shoes and of course, a broken thong.

The best sandals for the beach are those that meet the following criteria: They need to be comfortable, made out of beach-friendly materials (waterproof or water-resistant if you plan on getting them wet, breathable for hot, sweaty summer feet, and outfitted with a durable sole for walking on a variety of surfaces, from the sand to the boardwalk), functional for your beach activities of choice, and of course, stylish. Luckily, the beach sandal market has evolved since the days of rubber flip-flops. Here are the best beach sandals according to a top podiatrist, a few stylists, and an experienced beach bunny.

Best Overall

Reef Cushion Celine

Reef

What We Love: Water-friendly and durable, these flip-flops feel more expensive than they actually are.

What We Don't Love: While we love the neutrals, we wish it came in more fun color options.

Beach sandals need to work on both the sand and the boardwalk, which is why we’re fans of this cushioned sandal from Reef that comes highly recommended by stylist Clare Pollard. Pollard calls it her “tried-and-true brand” because they’re a bit more elevated than a typical flip-flop. (“The scallop edge adds a chic vibe making these so much more than just a basic flip flop,” she says.) But we wear them for comfort and functionality. The arch support ensures your foot still feels braced as you walk on notoriously uneven surfaces — something rare for such minimal shoes — and the water-friendly, vegan leather won’t be ruined if you inch too close to the water. Actually, you could even wear them in the water, if you wanted, and never have to worry about the sandal taking eons to dry or needing to be thrown out almost immediately. We wish they came in more bright, summer-friendly colors, but Pollard points out that the neutrals, especially the tan, “go with everything,” so you can wear them with nearly everything.

Size: 5–11 | Material: Vegan leather | Colors: 7

Best Budget

Ipamena Ana Flip Flop

Amazon

What We Love: These simple yet sturdy flip-flops are made with an anatomic footbed for premium support and comfort.

What We Don't Love: They run small.

Rubber flip-flops probably come instantly to mind when you think of sandals you can just slip into a beach bag before heading out. Great on sand and typically at an affordable price, the sandals won’t break your heart if they need to be tossed after a summer (or even just one vacation) of continuous wear. However, this pair from Ipanema won’t be destined for the trash bin anytime soon. The signature style from the Brazilian brand comes in endless color and pattern options (the glitter is our favorite) and lots of comfortable features. Made with a no-slip rubber sole, these babies are designed to last for years, so they won’t fall apart in the middle of your beach day. Unlike other basic flip flops, The footbed on these is anatomically molded, offering more arch support than other similar designs.

Size: 5–11 | Material: Rubber | Colors: 34

Best Splurge

Valentino Garavani Rockstud Jelly Sandal

Nordstrom

What We Love: Luxurious and timeless, these jelly sandals will take you from the beach to a night out on the town.

What We Don't Love: It’s best to size up because these run small.

If you plan on spending most of your time lounging on the beach instead of walking on it, Toronto-based stylist Kim Appelt suggests prioritizing style, like the Valentino Garavani Rockstud Jelly Sandal, an elevated beach shoe that is both fashionable and flexible.

The strappy gladiator design keeps feet secure, and the sole of the shoe features a pronounced tread to prevent you from slipping on wet surfaces. The addition of the studs also makes them a bit dressier, so you can easily wear them out for a nice lunch or an afternoon of shopping, too. “A good tip is to pick a sandal that is in a neutral color so it can go from the beach to dinner and will match many outfits,” Appelt says. Similar to the Ipanema flip-flops mentioned before, these shoes also aren’t offered in half sizes and run a bit small, so we recommend sizing up for the best fit possible.

Size: 5–11 | Material: PVC | Colors: 3

Most Comfortable

Birkenstock Birkenstock Unisex Arizona Essentials EVA

Nordstrom

What We Love: The contoured footbed absorbs shock and provides optimal arch support for pain-free walking.

What We Don't Love: They can feel a bit sticky when your feet sweat.

Dr. Brad Schaeffer, a New York City-based board-certified podiatrist and foot surgeon at Central Park SOLE stresses the importance of selecting a comfortable beach sandal that offers ample heel and arch support. While he says the original hemp and leather Birks offer the “sturdiest soles, original attention to the footbed (designed to anatomically fit the foot), and quality of materials,” the waterproof PVC version offers the same support, but they’re better suited for a day at the beach.

You can walk long lengths in the pair, but we found they got a bit sticky if your feet sweat while wearing them. That doesn’t impact the comfort, though, because the cushioned footbed is contoured to keep feet supported and absorb shock, ultimately fending off pain while walking across all sorts of terrain, including uneven sand. The adjustable uppers also offer another layer of support, ensuring feet stay snug and don’t slip around. They’re available in extended sizing to accommodate wider feet, and overall, we think they run large, so we recommend sizing down for the best fit.

Size: Regular/Wide, Medium/Narrow in whole sizes 4–12 | Material: PVC | Colors: 11

Best Luxury

Bottega Veneta Carpet Rubber Slide Sandals

Net-A-Porter

What We Love: These designer slides are constructed of thick rubber, making them more sturdy and long-lasting than other sandals on our list.

What We Don't Love: Because these slides are unisex, those with narrow feet might find them too wide.

If you are going to go designer, go all in with these durable rubber slides from Bottega Veneta, which generally wear better and last longer than flimsy sandals. Recommended by Pollard, the slides feature graphic embossed uppers, treaded soles, and a comfortable molded footbed. Pollard considers these “a feel-good splurge” because the dense rubber will hold up through multiple seasons with minimal wear and tear — even when worn on sand. While neutrals like white, beige and black are times, she suggests going for the green. “It’s neon which adds a fun pop to neutral colors in the summer.” The unisex sizing makes these a bit wider than other options, so they may not be the best for those with narrow feet.

Best Dressy

Saks Fifth Avenue

Whether you like to dress up for a day on the shoreline or have a summer beach wedding to attend, investing in a pair of elegant beach-friendly sandals should be on your list. Pollard recommends looking for metallics because they look more elevated than other colors and are neutral enough to go with everything. She is a huge fan of Ancient Greek Sandals, a brand she relies on for high-quality, handmade shoes.

This pair in particular features “metallic striped embroidery [that] makes them a perfect choice to compliment a dressy, fun, beach-friendly party ensemble,” she explains. “They also pair well day-to-day with denim shorts and a white tee.”

What to Keep in Mind

Materials

According to Dr. Schaeffer, the best materials for the summer should be flexible and breathable because “our feet sweat and swell,” he says. So look for natural fibers, cotton blends, woven materials that allow for airflow yet aren’t too restrictive. “Quick dry materials are great for long walks, the beach, and even hiking.” Osemwenkhae adds that sandals made of plastic or raffia like the Freedom Moses, Birkenstock and even the J.Crew espadrille are great because “if they get a little sand on them because it just slides right off.”

Brown adds that a rubber base “or at least rubber sole” (like the one found on the Ancient Greek Kentima Slides) will be the most practical for the beach. “Canvas will show wear and tear and leather doesn’t hold up as well when wet, so any sort of rubber or plastic is going to be optimal,” she adds.

And, beware of adornments, warns Osemwenkhae. “When buying beach sandals, you always want to look for sandals with minimal or no hardware for comfort when exposed to the sun,” she notes because metal buckles might heat up and burn you (yes, seriously) when you put them on again. Also, if a gem falls off in the sand, too, you likely will never find it again, so keep the footwear minimal.

Support

“Beach sandals have come a long way, especially over the past few years,” explains Dr. Schaeffer. “It is easy to find stylish and fun summer and beach sandals, of all types now, that won’t exacerbate existing conditions or damage our feet."

As with any shoe, it is important to look for an appropriate level of support and comfort to stabilize your feet, whether a flip-flop, dress sandal, or even walking/hiking sandals, says Dr. Schaeffer. “The most important thing to consider when looking for sandals is support, for both the arch and heel.” The Vionic and Birkenstock meet both the criteria.

Brown points out that if you will be “kicking off your shoes when you hit the sand,” support isn’t as crucial. However, make sure they fit well. “Comfort is always important, and keep in mind that your feet swell in the heat so anything that starts off tight will feel even tighter by the end of the day,” she adds.

Beach Activity

You should also consider the type of activity you plan on wearing your sandals for. For example, for vigorous walking on rougher terrain, you should opt for sandals with heavier-duty treads, says Dr. Schaeffer. The Teva sandal is a great option for anyone who plans on being incredibly active on the beach, since they offer the most supportive straps and ample treads.

“For hiking and beach sports, you’ll want sandals that hold your foot as stable as possible – not flip-flops,” he points out. “Adjustable straps are great because as feet get wet, they will slide around, and this is what will cause blisters, cuts, and overall discomfort.” 

Style Preferences

Finally, consider your own personal style, says Appelt. For example, if you aren’t a fan clunkier sandals, you should probably stay away from Birkenstocks or Crocs. Also, you should try and choose a style and color that will work with the rest of your wardrobe.

Your Questions, Answered

What footwear is best for the beach?

Bottom line: While there is no perfect one-style-fits-all sandal, the best sandal for the beach depends entirely on your personal needs and wants. However, make sure to look for a sandal that is “waterproof or water-resistant and lightweight,” says Appelt, offers the “appropriate level of support and comfort to stabilize your feet,” adds Dr. Schaeffer, and has a sole comfortable enough for “leisure walks on the beach,” notes Osemwenkhae.“At the end of the day, whether you opt for a slide, flip-flop or strappy sandal, the best beach shoe is the one that makes you and your feet happy all day long,” Pollard points out.
