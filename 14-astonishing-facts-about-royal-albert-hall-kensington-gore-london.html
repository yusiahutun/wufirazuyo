The Royal Albert Hall was opened by Queen Victoria in 1871.

With a seating capacity of over 5,000 people, this magnificent concert hall was named in memory of Prince Albert, Queen Victoria’s beloved husband.

Over 1.7 million people attend events at the Royal Albert Hall every year.

The world-famous Proms concerts are held at the Royal Albert Hall.

The Proms is a series of classical music concerts that take place every summer at the Royal Albert Hall. It is one of the most celebrated classical music festivals in the world.

The Royal Albert Hall has a resident organ.

The Grand Organ, installed in 1871, is one of the largest pipe organs in the world. It contains over 9,999 pipes and is a spectacular feature of the hall’s interior.

Winston Churchill’s funeral was held at the Royal Albert Hall.

In 1965, this historic venue played host to the state funeral of the iconic British Prime Minister, Winston Churchill. The event was attended by dignitaries from around the world.

The Beatles performed at the Royal Albert Hall.

In 1963, the legendary band performed at the Royal Albert Hall, marking a memorable moment in their illustrious career. Their performance was met with enthusiastic acclaim from fans.

The ceiling of the Royal Albert Hall is adorned with beautiful mosaics.
