Accountant salary in Kenya in 2024: Earnings, ranks, and qualifications

Accountants are qualified personnel with skills in managing, analyzing and inspecting financial accounts in a company. These professionals are tasked with creating sales and cash flow reports, administering payroll, carrying out billing activities, keeping balance sheets, managing budgets and keeping an inventory, among other things. So, what is the accountant salary in Kenya in 2024?

Many children claim they want to be accountants when they grow up. This dream is valid with hard work and determination. Although the accounting field is flooded, it is one of the top-paying jobs in Kenya if you find yourself up the corporate ladder.

Each rank has a set salary scale that is reviewed periodically depending on your company. Most of the well-paid accountants in Kenya are those working as chief accountants or managing governmental financial institutions and large private financial institutions such as (NGOs).

The monthly salary of an accountant in Kenya depends on various factors, such as the candidates' level of education and academic qualification, years of experience, gender, and location, among other factors. Experience level and academic qualifications are the most important factors in determining the salary. The more years of experience, the higher your wage.

For instance, if you have a master's degree and a CPA, you are likely to get a job in big companies, most of which pay well. However, you must have relevant job experience, sometimes up to 5 years.

Accountant salary in Kenya ranges between Kshs. 35000 and Kshs. 50000 for CPA (K) holders. This is inclusive of housing and transport allowances, among other benefits. Below are salaries for different categories of accountants.

1. Chief accountant salary

A chief accountant is also known as a principal accounting officer. They are responsible for the preparation of financial and performance reports. Additionally, the chief accountant oversees all the company's accounting undertakings. A person working as a chief accountant in Kenya typically earns approximately Ksh 160,000 monthly.

Salaries range between Ksh 83,100 (lowest) and 245,000 KES (highest). However, for big companies, these professionals can earn between Ksh. 852,000 and Ksh 2,353,120 on average.

To be a chief accountant, you require a bachelor's degree in a relevant field, QuickBooks ProAdvisor certification, and a minimum of seven years of experience as a CPA in a corporate setting. A master's degree, PhD, and fellowship programs are always an added advantage.

2. Chartered accountant

A chartered accountant is an international accounting designation granted to accounting professionals in Kenya. Their roles include auditing and assurance, financial accounting and reporting, and management accounting, among other duties. A chartered accountant makes approximately between Ksh 71,900 and Ksh 212,000 per month.

3. Senior accountant salary

Senior accountants earn an average salary of between Ksh 100,000 and Ksh 199,000 per month. To become a senior accountant, you must have a bachelor's degree in a relevant field, at least three years of experience and a certified public accountant (CPA) license in good standing.

4. Bank accountant salary in Kenya

A bank accountant is responsible for overseeing the financial accounts of a banking institution. A bank accountant's salary in Kenya ranges from Ksh. 34,000 to Ksh. 124,000 per month.

5. Junior accountant earnings

This is the lowest level a graduate begins from a training institution. The salary of a junior accountant depends on the employer. However, a junior accountant's salary ranges between Ksh. 19 478 and Ksh. 71, 239 on average.

6. Assistant accountant salary

These professionals are designed to help the general accountant in carrying out their duties in managing and analyzing the financial systems of the company. They pocket a monthly salary of between Ksh. 39,000 and Ksh. 135,000.

How much is the accountants' salary in Nairobi?

An accountant in Nairobi earns about Ksh 111,000 per month compared to their counterparts in other famous cities like Kisumu and Mombasa, who earn approximately Ksh 95,400 and Ksh 103,000, respectively.
